KLID is an American radio station licensed to Poplar Bluff, Missouri, broadcasting on 1340 kHz AM. The station airs a format consisting of News/Talk and Sport/Talk and is owned by Browning Skidmore Broadcasting, Inc. KLID is the "Voice of NASCAR in Poplar Bluff and has been the Voice of the Poplar Bluff Mules for 55 consecutive years.

KLID was founded and named for a brilliant broadcaster and broadcast engineer Don Lidenton. He successfully created the most popular station in Poplar Bluff's history, the first Top 40 formatted station in South East Missouri. KLID former DJs include Scott Innes who was the voice of Scooby Doo.
