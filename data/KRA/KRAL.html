KRAL is an AM radio station licensed to Rawlins, Wyoming and broadcasting at 1240 kHz. The station simulcasts sister station KIQZ's programming, which was primarily a 90s/2000s/Now AC format. It was developed in the 1950s under the supervision of William D. "Bill" McCraken, a Wyoming radio and television pioneer. The station, as of July 2010, was silent, while its sister FM counterpart remained on air for a short period of time. The radio stations returned to broadcast status in January 2016 under Federal Communications Commission special temporary authority.
