KXST (1140 kHz) is a commercial AM radio station licensed to North Las Vegas, Nevada, and broadcasting to the Las Vegas metropolitan area. The station is owned by Entercom, and airs a sports radio format as part of the CBS Sports Radio network. The studios and offices are located in the unincorporated Clark County area of Spring Valley. In April 2018, KXST became the official Las Vegas affiliate of the Los Angeles Dodgers Radio Network.

KXST's transmitter is near Nellis Air Force Base and Interstate 15, on East Tropical Parkway. It is powered at 10,000 watts by day, using a non-directional antenna. But AM 1140 is a clear channel frequency reserved for XEMR Monterrey, Mexico and WRVA Richmond, Virginia. So at night, to reduce its interference, KXST cuts power to 2,500 watts and uses a directional antenna. KXST is not licensed by the FCC to broadcast in the HD Radio digital format on the AM dial. On the FM dial, KXST is heard on 107.5 KXTE-HD3.
