KEYJ-FM, Rock 108 is a commercial radio station located in Abilene, Texas, broadcasting on 107.9 FM. KEYJ-FM airs a mainstream rock music format branded as "Rock 108". Before 1979 however, the call letters KEYJ were assigned to a station located in Jamestown, North Dakota, broadcasting at 1400 AM, (now KQDJ), which gained brief fame in 1957, when it was featured in Life Magazine, as the home of the "World's Youngest D-J"; the now world-famous Shadoe Stevens.

KEYJ won the RadioContraband Rock Radio Award for the "Under the Radar" station of the Year in 2014.
