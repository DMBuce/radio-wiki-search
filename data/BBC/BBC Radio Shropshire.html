BBC Radio Shropshire is the BBC Local Radio service for the English county of Shropshire. Launched on 23 April 1985, it was the 30th BBC local station. It originally broadcast on both FM and AM (on 756 kHz and 1584 kHz), but changed to FM only in 1991. The 756 kHz AM frequency was later allocated to Radio Maldwyn, based in Newtown, Powys, ready for their launch of 'The Magic 756' in 1993.

The station now broadcasts from its studios in Shrewsbury on 96 MHz (for central and north Shropshire), 90 MHz (Church Stretton), 95 MHz (in Ludlow), 104.1 MHz (in Clun) FM and DAB (digital radio).

According to RAJAR, the station has a weekly audience of 109,000 listeners and a 12.4% share as of December 2018.
