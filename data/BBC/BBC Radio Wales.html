BBC Radio Wales is a Welsh national radio station operated by the BBC. It began broadcasting on 13 November 1978, following the demise of the 'Radio 4 Wales' service (previously the Welsh Home Service) and BBC Radio 4's transformation into a UK-wide network, moving from medium wave to long wave. Radio Wales broadcasts in English, whilst sister network Radio Cymru has Welsh language programming.

The station's managing editor is Colin Paterson.

According to RAJAR, the station has a weekly audience of 316,000 listeners and a listening share of 4% as of December 2018.
