BBC Radio 5, a BBC radio network, carried sport, children's and educational programmes from 1990 to 1994.

It transmitted via analogue radio on 693 and 909Â kHz, and lasted for three years and eight months. The success of BBC Radio 4's coverage of the Gulf War of 1990-1991, on a service nicknamed "Scud FM", demonstrated the popularity of a 24-hour news service. A rolling news and sport station, named BBC Radio 5 Live, replaced Radio 5 on 28 March 1994.
