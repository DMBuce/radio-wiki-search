KWAM (990 kHz) is a commercial AM radio station in Memphis, Tennessee. The station airs a talk radio format and is owned by Todd Starnes’ Starnes Media Group. The studios and offices are on Murray Road in Memphis. The transmitter is located off Bridgeport Road in Marion, Arkansas.

By day, KWAM broadcasts with 10,000 watts. But because AM 990 is a Canadian clear-channel frequency, the station must reduce power at night to 450 watts. To allow listeners in Memphis to hear the station on FM, KWAM is simulcast on translator station W300DE at 107.9 MHz.

KWAM has a colorful history at the time as KWEM (1946–1959), helping "break" artists such as Elvis Presley, B.B. King, Johnny Cash, Ike Turner and Howlin' Wolf in the 1950s and 60s.
