WKKY is a commercial FM radio station in Geneva, Ohio, USA, broadcasting at 104.7 MHz with a country music format. The station broadcasts using the HD Radio format.

The station was started by Donald E. Martin on 28 April 1982 as WDON. On 16 September 1987, Martin organized Ray-Mar Broadcasting Company, and assigned to the station to the company, which was subsequently turned over to Warren T. Jones who is the son of Theodore Jones in Chardon. The station's call letters were changed to WKKY on 10 July 1992. Ray-Mar changed its name to Music Express Broadcasting Corporation of Northeast Ohio on 9 August1994.
