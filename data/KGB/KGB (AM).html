KGB (760 AM) is a commercial radio station in San Diego, California. It has a talk radio format and is owned by iHeartMedia, Inc. KGB serves San Diego County.

Signing on in 1941 as KFMB, the station picked up its present talk radio format in 1994 after many years as a music station. Throughout its history, KFMB featured hosts and personalities including Geoff Edwards, Bobby Rich, Ted Leitner, Rick Roberts, and Roger Hedgecock. From the 1970s to 2000s, KFMB was the radio home of various San Diego sports teams, namely the San Diego Chargers, San Diego Padres, and San Diego State Aztecs. Today, KGB features conservative talk shows both local and national, with Armstrong & Getty and The Mark Levin Show among its nationally syndicated programming.

From 1964 to 2018, KFMB-AM-FM-TV (AM 760, FM 100.7 and TV channel 8) were all owned by Midwest Television. In 2018, Tegna Inc. bought all three KFMB stations. In 2020, the radio stations were sold to Local Media San Diego, which then sold the AM station to iHeartMedia. Tegna continues to own KFMB-TV 8. Local Media San Diego changed the FM call sign to KFBG.
