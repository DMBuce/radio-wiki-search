WLKR (1510 kHz) branded 92.9 WLKR Classic, is a commercial AM radio station in Norwalk, Ohio. It airs a classic hits radio format and is owned by Elyria-Lorain Broadcasting Co. The studios are in Milan.

WLKR is a daytimer station, powered at 500 watts, using a directional antenna. But because AM 1510 is a clear channel frequency reserved for Class A WLAC Nashville, WLKR must sign-off at sunset. The transmitter is at the intersection of Huber Road and Lamereaux Road just outside of Norwalk. In addition to a standard analog transmission, WLKR simulcasts over FM translator W225DG at 92.9 MHz. The translator continues to broadcast all night, even after WLKR is dark. In its advertising and logo, WLKR uses its FM dial position.
