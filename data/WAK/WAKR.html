WAKR (1590 AM) – branded Soft Hits 93.5 FM – is a commercial full service/soft adult contemporary radio station licensed to serve Akron, Ohio. Locally owned by Rubber City Radio Group, Inc., the station covers the Akron metro area, and is the local affiliate for: CBS News Radio; the Cleveland Browns, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Cleveland Indians radio networks; as well as the Ohio State Sports Network. The WAKR studios are located in Akron's Wallhaven neighborhood, while the station transmitter is located in the city's southeast side. Besides a standard analog transmission, WAKR simulcasts over low-power Akron FM translator W228EL (93.5 FM), and is available online.

While not the first radio station to have been built in Akron, nor the oldest surviving station, WAKR has had the most measured historical impact of any broadcast outlet in the city, especially during their first 50 years of operation. During its creative and ratings pinnacle in the 1940s and 1950s, WAKR employed a succession of announcers who would find greater success elsewhere, most notably Alan Freed, Scott Muni and Art Fleming. Founded by S. Bernard Berk and operated for many years by his son, Roger G. Berk, WAKR's sustained success manifested itself in FM adjunct WAKR-FM (97.5) and TV outlet WAKR-TV (channel 49, later 23), culminating as the flagship of a nine-station broadcast chain in Akron, Dayton, Dallas and Denver. Owned by the Rubber City Radio Group since 1993, WAKR has changed formats six times in that time frame; the most recent change took place on January 24, 2020.
