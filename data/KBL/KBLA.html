KBLA (1580 kHz) is a commercial AM radio station licensed to Santa Monica, California, and serving the Greater Los Angeles area. The station is owned by Multicultural Broadcasting, headed by Arthur Liu. It airs a Spanish language Christian talk and teaching radio format known as "Radio Esperanza 1580 AM" (Radio Hope). TV personality Tavis Smiley is in the process of buying KBLA to launch a progressive talk format on the station.

KBLA broadcasts with a power of 50,000 watts day and night, using a directional antenna. The transmitter is off North Alvarado Street, near Glendale Boulevard, in Los Angeles. Most of the station's signal is directed over the Pacific Ocean to the west, to avoid interfering with KQFN in Phoenix, Arizona. In addition, 1580 AM is a Canadian clear channel frequency, further restricting KBLA's signal to the north. Consequently, KBLA can be heard at night in Hawaii, via AM nighttime skywave skip. But listeners in Riverside County and parts of Orange County may have trouble receiving KBLA's signal.
