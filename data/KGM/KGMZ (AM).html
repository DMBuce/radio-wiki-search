KGMZ (1550 AM; "Channel Q") is a commercial LGBTQ-oriented talk and electronic dance radio station licensed to serve San Francisco, California. Owned by Entercom, the station services the San Francisco Bay Area but does not originate any local programming, instead functioning as a repeater for Entercom's Channel Q service, with the transmitter located in the nearby suburb of Belmont. In addition to a standard analog transmission, KGMZ is simulcast over the second HD subchannel of co-owned KLLC and is available online via Radio.com.

Established in 1947 at San Mateo as KSMO, the station ran into a turbulent history between 1951 and 1961 with a myriad of financial and labor union issues. As KKHI between 1961 and 1994, it and FM adjunct KKHI-FM 95.7 distinguished themselves as the market's fine art/classical music voice for the Bay Area. While having undergone multiple format changes since then under subsequent owners Westinghouse Broadcasting, Infinity Broadcasting, CBS Radio and Entercom, the station—while as KYCY—also became the first terrestrial radio station to devote the entire broadcast day to playing podcasts in 2005.
