WCRW is a Chinese News/Talk formatted broadcast radio station licensed to Leesburg, Virginia, serving Metro Washington, D.C.. WCRW is owned by Potomac Radio Group, Inc. and leased full-time by Chinese government-owned China Radio International, a designated "foreign mission" in the U.S.

1190 AM is a Mexican and United States clear-channel frequency. XEWK in Zapopan, Jalisco, Mexico and KEX in Portland, Oregon are the Class A stations on this frequency. WOWO in Ft. Wayne, Indiana is a former Class A station which was downgraded to Class B after its purchase by Inner City Broadcasting in New York, New York; this allowed WLIB to remain on the air 24 hours per day.
