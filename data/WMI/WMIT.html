WMIT (106.9 MHz, "106.9 The Light") is a non-profit FM radio station licensed to Black Mountain, North Carolina. WMIT is a listener-supported ministry of the Billy Graham Evangelistic Association. It airs a mix of Contemporary Christian music with some Christian talk and teaching programs, including national religious leaders Jim Daly, John MacArthur, David Jeremiah, Chuck Swindoll and Charles Stanley. Studios and offices are on Porters Cove Road in Asheville. WMIT's tower rises 180 feet (55 m) above 6,557-foot (1,999 m) Clingman's Peak.

In 2007, WMIT began broadcasting in the HD Radio format, adding "theEdge 106.9" on the HD2 channel, featuring Christian rock music for teenagers and young adults, which is also heard on translator W292CJ (106.3 FM) in Asheville. In addition, WMIT is heard on translator station W234CF (94.7 FM) in Boone, North Carolina, and most of WMIT's schedule is simulcast on WFGW in the Knoxville, Tennessee, radio market.
