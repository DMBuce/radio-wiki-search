WSWN (900 AM) is a radio station formerly broadcasting a talk format branded as "Talk of the Palm Beaches." It was better known in the South Florida and Treasure Coast areas as "Sugar 900," named after the surrounding sugar cane industry in the Belle Glade and Pahokee area. The station is licensed to serve 18 counties across South Florida in the United States, and the Caribbean.

The station is currently owned by JVC Media, LLC, through licensee JVC Broadcasting, LLC. It was purchased along with sister FM station 93.5 FM on September 29, 2014, and the studios moved to Palm Beach Gardens along with WSVU/960 and two FM translators (W240CI at 95.9 FM in West Palm Beach and a Jupiter-based translator W295BJ at 106.9 FM) broadcasting the syndicated adult contemporary format (Scott Shannon's True Oldies Channel) of 960AM.
